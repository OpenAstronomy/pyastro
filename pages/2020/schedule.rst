Below are some notes on some important aspects of the conference program:

*Unconference sessions*
  During these slots, participants can run discussions, hacks, sprints,
  tutorials or similar sessions, which they can propose during the pitches.
  These will run in parallel and will be selected from the proposed sessions
  each day.

*Lightning talks*
  These will provide an opportunity for attendees to make short (3-5 minutes)
  presentations on any topic.

*Hack day*
  Thursday's schedule is deliberately free of talks and unconferences to allow
  attendees to spend the whole day hacking on a project of their choice.
  Rough ideas for projects can be presented for discussion on Wednesday
  afternoon, and there will be time to discuss ideas and choose a project on
  Thursday morning after more detailed pitches. Hacking traditionally continues
  into the evening after the conference dinner. There will also be time to tidy
  up any unfinished projects for presentation on Friday.
